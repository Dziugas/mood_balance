I come from Lithuania and my real name is hard to pronounce for most foreigners. It stands for “Joyful” - a trick of fate, I sometimes think to myself. An introvert to the bone, I do not like too much attention. And since the topic of this blog is somewhat sensitive I decided to go under a nickname. So, please, just call me Jimmy. At least for now, hope you understand.

I am 33 years old and I have been dealing with depressive states, anxiety, mood swings, recurring thoughts and symptoms of OCD since I was a teenager. Needless to say, all these problems affected my results in studies, work and relationships with people I love - my family, friends, girlfriends and sometimes even random folks.

For years I tried to heal myself with alcohol, weed, sleep and similar escapism. It was kind of OK during the normal states of my existence, yet during the life’s ups and downs, these habits would swing my feelings to extreme highs and lows making me inactive, unfocused, easily irritated, depressed and helpless.

A few years ago, when I was going through a very low patch, my mother encouraged me to seek help. I started therapy and was lucky to meet a doctor who understood me. Now I am sober and more in control of my life. Being me still ain’t a candy, but now I can deal with life’s challenges with more ease and self-esteem. My doctor calls my package of problems Cyclothymia.

Mood Balance is my blog about reaching for the joy in life. I will write and share links about things that help me deal with Cyclothymia - therapy, staying sober, yoga, meditation, sports, food or taking a walk once in a while when nothing else helps.

Disclaimer: this site is not for whining about how hard mine or anybody else's state of being is. Yes, life is hard sometimes. For everyone. Yet, here I want us to focus on the good things.
